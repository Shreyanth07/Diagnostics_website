Covid 19

What is COVID-19?

A coronavirus is a kind of common virus that causes an infection in your nose, sinuses, or upper throat. Most coronaviruses aren't dangerous. COVID-19 is a disease caused by SARS-CoV-2 that can trigger what doctors call a respiratory tract infection. It can affect your upper respiratory tract (sinuses, nose, and throat) or lower respiratory tract (windpipe and lungs).

Symptoms of COVID

The main symptoms of COVID includes Fever, Coughing, Shortness of breath, Fatigue, Body Aches, Headache, Sore Throat, Loss of Taste, Nausea and Diarrhea.

Preventive Measures to avoid COVID

Avoid non-essential travelling | wash your hands regularly for 20minutes | wear a cloth face mask | Avoid close contact | Maintain Social Distancing | Disinfect your belongings | Avoid touching your eyes, nose, and mouth when outside

When to consult a doctor

If you have COVID-19 symptoms or you've been in contact with someone diagnosed with COVID-19, contact your doctor or clinic right away for medical advice. Tell your health care team about your symptoms and possible exposure before you go to your appointment. Emergency signs and symptoms can include: Trouble Breathing | Persistent chest pain or pressure | Inability to stay awake | New confusion | Blue lips or face.
